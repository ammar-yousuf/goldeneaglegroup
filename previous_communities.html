Our previous communities


Golden Eagle is a leader in architectural design and construction. After achieving prominence in the industry for landmark commercial buildings, the focus was turned to land development and the creation of unique planned communities. The result of this vast experience is a better home for you and your family.

Golden Eagle provides superior homes and communities capturing the lifestyle you want and crafting more into your home than ordinary homebuilders. Commitment to the highest standards means you can rest assured that your home will have the level of luxury and comfort you expect. A wide variety of custom features and interior appointments allows you to turn your Golden Eagle home into a reflection of your personal style as it becomes your dream home.

Crafted with the finest materials, a Golden Eagle home is an outstanding value and an investment that enhances your lifestyle. Just like you, Golden Eagle is only satisfied with the best. The Golden Eagle team knows that no matter where you live, the place most important to you is your home.






Waverly Farm in Winchester, Virginia
Waverly Farm is a 330 acre dairy farm just north of the town of Winchester. This beautiful property has gently rolling topography with pastoral views of surrounding farms and countryside. Waverly Farm is subdivided into 66 single family home sites ranging in size from 2 to 6 acres. 152 acres of this property is dedicated to rural preservation while the home sites are on the remaining 178 acres.




Little River Farms in Aldie, Virginia
Little River Farms is 114 acre property which was subdivided into 21 home sites ranging in size from 3 to 10 acres.  This community has gorgeous mountain views, rolling hills and forested areas.  Homes sold there in the range of $1.5 Million.



Iman Estates in Great Falls, Virginia
Iman Estates is a custom designed, 5 homes community in an area that is considered by many to be a suburb of Washington, DC.  Homes in this small community sold from $3 to $12 Million.  Great Falls was named #1 of the 25 top-earning towns in the US by Money Magazine. 


Hamilton Heights in Hamilton, Virginia
Hamilton Heights is a community of 13 homes with gorgeous countryside views and a private pond.  The home sites were engineered and developed by Golden Eagle Group and then sold to Carlington Homes.


Beauregard Overlook and Beauregard Heights in Leesburg, Virginia
Beauregard is a two phased community of 57 homes situated in a beautiful wooded area near the Premium Outlets.  Golden Eagle developed the property, then sold and built the homes.  This community of homes ranging in size up to 4,500 SF was designed for upwardly mobile young professionals and homes were sold in the $900 Thousands. 


Forest Heights in Herndon, Virginia
Forest Heights is a community consisting of 12 homes on beautiful one-acre wooded lots.  House sizes are upwards from 5,500 SF.   The homes in this community were sold in the $1.6 Million range.


Roxbury Heights in Leesburg, Virginia
Roxbury Heights sits atop a bluff overlooking Leesburg.  The natural beauty of the area contributed to Golden Eagle’s decision to conserve several acres of the property for The Audubon Society.  Golden Eagle built and sold 10 homes, on 3 to 5 acre home sites, ranging in price upwards from $850 Thousand and remodeled the large estate home that was part of the original land purchase.


Light Ridge in Round Hill, Virginia
Light Ridge is a community of 27 beautiful homes with a charming view of the Blue Ridge Mountains.  The home sites were engineered and developed by Golden Eagle Group and then sold the property to Ryan Homes.


Stuart Hills Estates in Herndon, Virginia
This community presented great challenges to Golden Eagle to develop.  There was no access to public water or sewer on site.  The Golden Eagle team was able to work with landowners and Fairfax County to bring in the necessary infrastructure so the community could be completed.  Golden Eagle Group engineered and developed the 18 home sites and then sold the property to NV Homes.


Stratford in Leesburg, Virginia
The Stratford community has been called “the gate of Leesburg”.  Loudoun County zoned this property a PRC (Planned Residential Community).  The project consisted of 228 acres planned to build a total of 1,329 residential units.  Golden Eagle built a 12-acre lake for the enhancement of this community.  Golden Eagle purchased the first two phases of the community, developed the lots for 109 townhouses and 84 single family houses and then sold those lots to Ryan Homes.


Individual Custom Home in Aldie, Virginia
This custom home was built on an estate of 400 acres with a beautiful lake.  It took Golden Eagle two and a half years to complete this home that contains over 15,000 SF of finished living space.  The interior showcases over 9,000 SF of Italian marble, has a 35’ dome ceilinged living room and many other one of a kind custom features.


Vale Ridge Estate in Oakton, Virginia
This exclusive single family community of custom homes was built with a high level of detail and architectural craftsmanship.  This was one of Golden Eagle Group’s first forays into single family custom homes.


Walney Mills at Sully Station, Centreville, Virginia
Golden Eagle Group entered into a partnership with Christopher Corporation to develop and build this town house community.   Pictured is the Model Home used to sell the community.  This enclave is part of the last phase of Sully Station, a very large planned community in Fairfax County, Virginia. 




8500 Leesburg Pike, Tysons Corner, Virginia
This office building has over 60,000 SF of space.  Mr. Totonji handled the renovation and tenant build out of this building.  He was also overseer during the lease processing and management of the building.


1212 New York Avenue, Washington DC
This 170,000 SF building is located on the east end of the Central Business District and was completed in 1987.  The cost of land and construction was approximately $24 Million.  The building is currently owned by an affiliated company and is fully leased with a value in excess of $30 Million.


The Hamilton Building, Washington DC
This 120,000 SF building was originally constructed in the 1920’s as the Hamilton Hotel.  It was converted to office use in the 1970’s.   The renovation project was acquired in 1987.  It was the last site available on Franklin Square and is almost fully leased to the General Services Administration (GSA).  The project was sold to an investor in 1989 at an appraised value of $28 Million.


815 Connecticut Avenue, Washington DC
This 190,000 SF building, located in the heart of Washington’s Central Business District, represents one of the Capital’s most prestigious addresses.  Leased by some of the Nation’s preeminent law firms such as McKenzie, Clifford and Warnecke, this building was completely renovated at a cost of $3.5 Million.  After renovation, the building was purchased for $18.5 Million and has a renovated value of $25 Million.


1620 L Street, Washington DC
This 154,000 SF building, with 3 levels of underground parking, is located in Washington’s Central Business District.  The property was constructed at a cost of $40 Million by the Donahoe Companies under the direct supervision of Mr. Totonji.  Upon completion of construction and fully leased, the building has a value of over $70 Million.


Diplomatic Quarters Development, Riyadh, Saudi Arabia

This project was an immense land development project overseen by Mr. Totonji under Riyadh Development Authority in the Saudi Government office to transfer all embassies from Jedda to Riyadh.  Land in Riyadh was to be converted into a working area where the diplomatic quarters of various nations would have their embassies and residences.  Given the sensitive nature of the data and people there, the area was protected by a 40’ berm with materials being trucked in from other locations.  Additionally, the infrastructure included the roads, electrical grids, water supply, sewage system, water towers and drainage system.  Mr. Totonji was responsible for directing and inspecting the diplomatic quarters infrastructure.  He worked very closely with a huge team of American, British, Korean and Japanese workers from 15 companies to accomplish this herculean task.  The construction was completed in 1987.  Total expense for this project was over $500 Million.  Mr. Totonji considers building this city from desert soil one of his life’s greatest accomplishments.
